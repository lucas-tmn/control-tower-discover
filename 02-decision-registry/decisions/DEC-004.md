| D5 Undocumented assumptions / **confirmed distortion** | **(1) MOS Case 1/111 use `AVERAGEX` over per-item ratios, not a volume-weighted total.** Worked example from the live analysis: two items — A (100 excess / 10 demand → 10.0 months) and B (10 excess / 90 demand → 0.11 months) — average to **5.06 months**, while the true volume-weighted ratio is **1.1 months**. The published MOS number is **systemically inflated by low-volume, high-excess items**, which could make leadership believe excess is less urgent than it is, or vice versa depending on portfolio mix. **(2) Oversea Vendor classification** flags *all* non-Vietnam vendors as returnable, plus two specific Vietnam vendor IDs (633312, 643509) as exceptions — with no check for actual contractual returnability, MOQ, or return-program participation. **(3) Prod_Type (CG/UPH/RP)** is a hardcoded SQL CASE list; any new product class not explicitly listed silently falls into "RP," skewing the Open Capacity figures for both RP and the category the new class should have joined. **(4) Three different date-anchoring formulas** are used across `Inventory`/`SI at US Warehouse` (next-Saturday), `W2 SS Change` (yesterday, with a Monday-specific adjustment), and `Inv Age` (`GETDATE()` directly) — if refresh timing falls awkwardly relative to these anchors, the safety-stock-change figure and the main inventory position can reflect different underlying weeks without any visible warning. |
