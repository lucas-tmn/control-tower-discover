## Definition

**Resultant Forecast** is the final demand forecast quantity produced by Logility after planner-applied overrides and adjustments have been layered on top of the base statistical model output. It represents demand planning's approved forecast — the number the supply plan is expected to cover.

## What "Resultant" Means

Logility's planning workflow distinguishes between two values:

- **Statistical (base) forecast** — quantity generated by Logility's demand model from historical patterns; no human input applied
- **Resultant forecast** — the statistical forecast after planners have applied manual adjustments or overrides (e.g., new product launch lifts, event adjustments, management inputs)

The `ResultantForecast` column in [Current Forecast](/datasets/tables/FactCurrentForecast.md) always reflects the **post-override** value. It is not the raw statistical model output.

## What Resultant Forecast Is Not

Resultant Forecast does **not** include promotional lift. Promo lift is tracked separately as `PromoLift` and combined at the `TotalForecast` level. See [Promo Lift](/glossary/promo_lift.md).

Resultant Forecast is also not the same as [Net Forecast](/glossary/net_forecast.md), which is the statistical forecast portion of the supply plan *net of firm customer orders* and floored at zero.
