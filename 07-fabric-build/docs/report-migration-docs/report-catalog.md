# Pre-Migration Report Catalog

## Act+Fcst by WNK & MILL Prod Resource

- This report serves the supply chain planning domain, specifically production capacity and demand planning for Ashley's domestic manufacturing facilities — Wanek (WNK, locations 900515/600039/900639) and Millennium (MILL, location 624556). It answers the question of whether available production capacity covers the combined demand signal (actual open orders + forward forecast) at the item-warehouse-production-resource level, week by week on a fiscal calendar. A secondary thread tracks vendor-shipped quantities from MAPICS manufacturing history, allowing planners to reconcile what has already shipped against upcoming ETD windows. The model is filtered throughout to the specific vendor/make-buy codes that identify Wanek and Millennium items, and uses a 56-day offset convention to convert customer-request-week dates into estimated-delivery-week dates.

## Act+Fcst vs Manufacturing

- This report serves domestic manufacturing capacity planning at two levels of the supply chain. The primary question is whether domestic U.S. finished goods production capacity can absorb the blended demand signal — actual orders for past fiscal months plus resultant and promotional forecast for future months. It then steps one level up the supply chain to ask the same question of Wanek and Millennium: because those plants produce the kits and components that feed domestic finished goods production, their capacity (tracked via `ProdCapacity`) and actual output (tracked via `VendorShipped`) must also be sufficient to support the projected demand. The 56-day ETD offset used throughout the model reflects the lead time between when Wanek/Millennium ship kits and components and when domestic plants convert them into finished goods for customer delivery. Dimension data (product details, warehouse master, fiscal calendar) is sourced from shared PowerBI Dataflows rather than direct warehouse queries.

## AFT_SI-SS_PSW

- This report supports the Ashley Furniture International (AFI) wholesale supply chain planning team and tracks weekly PSW (Production/Sourcing/Warehouse) supply plan health. Its primary focus is the SI-SS metric — Shippable Inventory minus Safety Stock — which indicates whether on-hand and incoming inventory is sufficient to cover safety stock requirements by item, warehouse, and week. The report lets planners monitor purchase order timelines from two perspectives: DUE date (warehouse receipt week) and ETD date (estimated time of departure from origin), with color-coded visual indicators (red, blue, purple, orange, etc.) driven by order type and SS% status. It also provides demand fulfillment rates across three views — against sold orders (CO), against shippable inventory (SI), and against safety stock (SS) — and supports week-over-week comparisons using a current snapshot versus a prior-week snapshot. Supplemental SharePoint files enrich the model with vendor attributes, EVC/Consumer Choice classifications, Wanek-specific DRP breakdowns, and an NFM item filter list.

## Amazon POS Sales and Forecast

- This report serves the eCommerce supply chain domain, specifically tracking Ashley's Amazon channel performance at the SKU level. It consolidates four Amazon-specific data streams — consumer point-of-sale (POS) activity, inventory positions at Amazon's fulfillment centers, Amazon's forward demand forecast, and Ashley's wholesale shipments to Amazon — enabling side-by-side comparison of sell-through, inventory health, and supply pipeline. The report appears to answer questions such as: How are our items actually selling on Amazon versus what we shipped? What is our current inventory position (on-hand, open POs, aged stock)? How does Amazon's forecast align with historical POS, and are we sizing our wholesale orders appropriately? The wholesale data is filtered to specific Amazon account numbers (3352200 and 3559000), suggesting the report is scoped to one or two Amazon vendor relationships. A calculated date filter (`z_DataRange`) bounds the visible date range from the earliest open wholesale order week through the latest forecast week end.

## Forecast Accuracy (ItWh)

- This report supports Ashley Furniture's supply chain demand planning team in evaluating **item-warehouse forecast accuracy** across five forecast horizons (2-week, 30-, 60-, 90-, and 120-day lookaheads). The core fact table (`ItWHAccy`) joins Logility demand forecasts from `SupplyChain_Enh.DemandForecastSnapshot` against actual demand from `SupplyChain_Enh.ItemActualDemand_Logility` and a naive benchmark derived from `SupplyChain_Enh.ActualsCustItemWH_AFI`, enabling comparison of model accuracy against a simple prior-period baseline. Key metrics — Bias %, wMAPE, MAPE, and naive wMAPE — can be sliced by product category, warehouse, vendor, and inventory segmentation (ABC/XYZ archetype via `InvSegGlobalCC`). The `Demand Planner Assignment Rules` and `Users` tables (sourced from SharePoint) associate collective product classes to individual demand planners for accountability reporting. Month-end status snapshots track item lifecycle stages (new, in-line, dropping, discontinued) to provide item-status context alongside accuracy metrics.

## Forecast Accuracy (Cust_ItWh)

- This report serves the Supply Chain Demand Planning domain at Ashley Furniture Industries (AFI), measuring forecast accuracy at the customer group, item SKU, and warehouse grain (Cust-Item-Warehouse). It evaluates forecast performance across four horizon windows (2-Week, 30-Day, 60-Day, 90-Day) by comparing the planning system's snapshots (sourced from `SupplyChain_Enh.DemandForecastSnapshot`) against actual demand (`ItemActualDemand_Logility`) and two baseline forecasts: a naive baseline (prior-month actuals scaled by week count) and an unspecified benchmark baseline (B). The primary metrics are directional bias (Bias%), weighted MAPE (wMAPE), and absolute error, with value-add measures quantifying improvement over both baselines. A structural split in the main query separates pre-March 2025 data (item-warehouse grain, customer group hardcoded as `AFICONS`) from post-March 2025 data (full customer-item-warehouse grain), with snapshot dates anchored to the 3rd Monday of each fiscal month and hardcoded cycle deviations for specific periods. Planner accountability by customer group and collective class is loaded from SharePoint, and product/warehouse dimensions are sourced from shared Power BI Dataflows.

## DvC - WanekMillenium

- This report tracks **Demand vs. Capacity (DvC)** for two furniture manufacturing vendors — **Wanek** (vendor codes 600039 / 900639) and **Millenium** (vendor code 624556 / 900515) — serving Ashley Furniture's supply chain planning team. It answers whether manufacturing capacity at each facility can absorb the planned, firm, and shipped unit volumes from the Production Schedule Workbench (PSW) over a rolling 30-week horizon. Key questions include: How do actual and promised capacity commitments compare to the constrained and unconstrained supply plan? Are actual production scans (FG Output and RP Scan from iSeries factory systems) tracking in line with PSW projections? Structurally, the model pulls PSW weekly snapshots and unconstrained plan from the Ashley EDW warehouse, enriches them with item classification from iSeries (item category, ITCLAD), and overlays capacity override/promise data from a manually-maintained SharePoint Excel workbook. A linear trendline layer across ETD weeks enables visual capacity trend analysis.

## GF Act+Fcst

- This model supports Global Furniture (GF) supply chain demand planning by combining three fact streams — historical actual demand snapshots (L1ACTD), current forecast snapshots (L1Fcst, with RSLF, PROL, and FUTO datatypes), and customer order history (OrderHist) — into a unified act+forecast view. It answers questions such as: How does actual demand compare to the rolling forecast? What is forecast consumption by customer, product, or warehouse? How is a given item or product line trending year-over-year? A significant portion of the measures support product lifecycle analysis (new, discontinued, and phase-drop items) and warehouse distribution split analysis. The `Fiscal Month Indicator` column in `z_FiscalCal` is a central filtering mechanism used throughout the measures to distinguish past, current, and future periods. Four dimension tables (`z_FiscalCal`, `z_ProductDetails`, `z_VendorMaster`, `z_WarehouseMaster`) are sourced from a shared Power BI Dataflow rather than directly from the SQL warehouse; `z_LeatherFlag` is sourced from a SharePoint-hosted Excel file and provides a supplemental leather-item flag for filtering.

## Inventory Health

- This model tracks inventory health and available-to-promise (ATP) metrics across Ashley Furniture's warehouse network. It integrates demand forecasts (both working and current supply forecasts), historical shipment patterns, safety stock targets, and dependent demand calculations to produce a weekly item-warehouse view of on-hand inventory status. The model classifies inventory into five health buckets (Below Max, Over Target, Excess, High Excess, and TIB/Throw in the Bay) based on days of supply and safety stock multiples. It also measures ATP in-stock rates by recent launch window to support operational planning and supply chain visibility.

## Inventory Transactions and Item Balance Detail

The model enables analysis of inventory flow, warehouse utilization by product category and storage type, and net inventory changes. Primary data sources are the ashley_edw warehouse (InventoryTransactionsWeeklySummary and ItemBalance tables) with supporting product and warehouse dimensions populated from Power BI Dataflows; the model includes only finished goods and specific configured item classes, filtered to the most recent 18 months by fiscal calendar.

## Plan Drop 1

- This is an end-of-life inventory projection model that combines actual historical demand with future forecasts to estimate stockout dates for items nearing end-of-life. Demand planners use the model to project when on-hand inventory will be exhausted given a combination of historical demand patterns (ACT_DEMD_0 through ACT_DEMD_11) and forward forecasts (Current Month FC through Month+11 FC). As a secondary function, the model highlights significant deviations between average historical monthly sales and the forward forecast; planners respond to these flagged deviations by updating the forecast, which automatically recalculates the projected stockout date. The data is filtered to specific forecast types (Plan, Weekly, and Forecast buckets) and always presents the latest snapshot from the planning system.

## Weekly Trend Analysis

- This model enables demand planners to track Ashley Furniture Industries (AFI) wholesale sales through multiple temporal lenses: invoiced (actual fulfillment), written (order entry as a leading indicator for demand spikes), and current request (forward-looking customer demand signals). By analyzing these three sales dimensions alongside rolling forecasts across a 26-week window, planners identify supply chain signals and take corrective action. When written or requested quantities outpace the forecast, demand planners should prepare for demand increases. When invoiced quantity lags behind written or requested quantity, it signals a growing backlog requiring supply chain intervention. Conversely, when the forecast substantially exceeds all three actual demand signals (invoiced, written, and requested), the planner should consider reducing the forecast to align with market reality.

## When to Disco v2

- This report tracks the end-of-life inventory depletion process for Ashley Furniture Industries (AFI), answering the critical question: "When to Discontinue" products from the catalog. By monitoring real-time inventory levels (on-hand, in-transit, on-order) and calculating months-of-supply, the report identifies when items fall below the two-week supply threshold—the trigger point for changing item status from "Current" to "Discontinued" and notifying sales teams of discontinuation. The model integrates warehouse inventory across multiple locations (with separate tracking for warehouse 335), customer allocated orders, forecast planner assignments, and product details (series, colors, status) to enable planners to manage orderly product exits and coordinate discontinuation notices with the sales organization.

## Demand Review

- The Demand Review semantic model supports **supply chain demand planning, inventory management, and forecast accuracy tracking** across Ashley Furniture's product portfolio and customer segments. It reconciles actual orders with forecasts (from Logility and internal planning systems), tracks inventory position (supply plan detail, ATP, on-hand quantities), and measures performance against consumption targets. The model's **primary purpose is to enable product review and demand consensus meetings**, where the organization reaches agreement on the forecast. Gaining consensus on demand is the critical first step in the integrated business planning process, commonly known as Sales, Inventory and Operations Planning (SIOP or S&OP), which aligns supply, demand, and financial plans across the organization. The model serves demand planners, forecast analysts, and supply chain leadership, enabling them to identify forecast bias patterns, inventory health risks, and demand-fulfillment gaps by product, warehouse, and customer group. Key features include adjustments for Restoration Hardware (RH) product lines, seasonal analysis, and embedded exception-detection measures that flag chronic forecasting issues or component imbalances in bedroom furniture sets.

## Product Review (NEW)

- This model supports the demand consensus process that is central to Ashley Furniture's Integrated Business Planning (IBP) initiative. It tracks forecast accuracy and bias across multiple time horizons (30/90-day), monitors inventory positioning and demand fulfillment rates across warehouses, and measures supply plan execution against customer demand. Key features include customer-item level forecast accuracy analysis, seasonal demand adjustment and trending, year-over-year growth comparisons, and real-time inventory status (on-hand, safety stock, excess). The model enables cross-functional stakeholders (demand planners, category analysts, supply chain managers) to collaboratively review forecast performance, identify forecast gaps and opportunities, and build consensus on demand signals that feed into supply, financial, and operational planning cycles. The model consolidates data from multiple sources including the EDW warehouse (order history, ATP), dataflows (fiscal calendar, product/vendor/warehouse masters), and non-warehouse sources (SharePoint planning lists, demand fulfillment dataflows) to provide the transparency and analytics needed for effective demand consensus meetings and decision-making.

## Safety Stock Analysis

- This model supports inventory planning and safety stock optimization across Ashley Furniture's warehouses by tracking inventory levels, status, and costs against safety stock targets. It answers questions about which items are overstocked or understocked, how well inventory levels match demand variance, and whether safety stock quantities achieve desired inventory turnover and cost goals. The model covers the full product lifecycle (current, planned drops, new items, discontinuations) and integrates forecast data, demand patterns, cost defaults, and fiscal calendars to enable weekly and month-end inventory reviews and variance analysis.

## Supplier On-Time Performance

- This report tracks supplier on-time delivery performance for Ashley Furniture's supply chain planning team, focused on international vendor shipping compliance. It measures whether vendors ship furniture items on time relative to firmed purchase order demand, using two delivery windows: a 3-week bucket (W3) and a 4-week bucket (W4) that can be toggled via a `TableSelection` slicer. The report quantifies delayed shipments by piece count and by container volume, calculates penalty amounts for shipments that arrive 2 or 3 weeks late (at 2% and 5% of VDP price respectively), and exposes a fiscal year-to-date on-time percentage. Two fact sources are combined: a PSW (Purchase Schedule Week) on-time fact from warehouse snapshots and an Ashton-specific vendor on-time table. The `Vendor List` (Excel via SharePoint) provides analyst assignments, and ABC coding classifies vendors by demand volume to prioritize high-impact suppliers.

## Supply Plan Detail

- This report supports the Supply Chain Planning team's weekly supply review. The central fact table (`SupplyPlanDetail`) is a daily snapshot extract from the Logility supply planning tool, representing the current day's plan. Each row captures expected supply movements for a single item-SKU × warehouse × week-ending date, covering the full inventory position: beginning balance, firm and planned production, purchase orders, transfers in/out, demand, and the derived Shippable Inventory (SI — weeks of supply on hand). The report answers whether each SKU and warehouse is above or below safety stock (SI-SS), where negative supply positions exist, and what production and POs are scheduled over the coming weeks. A forward-looking horizon of 35 weeks is modeled using a relative `Fiscal Week Indicator` (0 = current week), enabling coverage trending and capable-to-promise analysis. All dimension tables (fiscal calendar, product details, vendor master, warehouse master) are sourced from Power BI Dataflows rather than the warehouse.
