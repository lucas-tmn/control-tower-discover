# Discovery Vision

## Mission
Discover Ashley's Business Decision Landscape and externalize organizational
decision knowledge into trusted, reusable Decision Intelligence assets.

## The problem this exists to solve
Ashley makes hundreds of supply chain decisions every week — what to
forecast, what to replenish, what to escalate — but the knowledge behind
those decisions lives in three disconnected places: 26 semantic models built
independently over time, the heads of planners and buyers who have never
been asked to externalize their logic, and scattered SOPs/spreadsheets
nobody governs centrally.

## What Discovery is — and is not
We are not discovering reports. We are discovering **decisions**: what
triggers them, what data justifies them, what rules govern them, who owns
them, and what they cost when they go wrong. Reports, interviews, and SOPs
are three different windows onto the same underlying landscape — Discovery
reconciles what they show into one coherent map, rather than treating any
one of them as sufficient on its own.

## Why this matters now
The reporting estate has already been quantified as unsustainable: 26 Power
BI models, 879 measures, 10 exact-duplicate queries. That is the evidence for
sprawl. What has not yet been quantified is the *decision* sprawl behind
it — how many distinct decisions those reports actually serve, and whether
Ashley's people trust the same numbers when making them. That gap is
Discovery's job to close.

## Definition of success
By the end of Discovery, anyone at Ashley can ask "how is X decided today?"
and get one trusted answer, traceable to its evidence — not three
conflicting dashboards and a Slack thread.
